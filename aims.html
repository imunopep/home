Aims:


A1: Analysis of Tyr369-377 epitope oxidation and method for obtaining its oxidized forms
This aim will be accomplished during the first stage of the implementation, in the first nine months of the project. It involves kinetic studies of the epitope oxidation potential mainly using reversed-phase liquid chromatography coupled with UV-VIS detection. The separation method will be up-scaled to semi-preparative or preparative conditions in order to obtain sufficient quantitites of the peptide for the follow-up experiments.

A2: Immunological profiling of each YMD oxidative form
At this level the immunological recognition of specific HLA-YMD peptide complexes is assesed in collaboration with the group of prof. Pedro Romero from Department of oncology UNIL CHUV, Lausanne University, Switzerland. This allows the extension of a previous succesfull colaboration with the same group (more details: here). Also, HLA binding specificity will be addresed in wet-lab experiments performed in IBRA.

A3: Evaluation of the oxidation reaction as a potential intracellular process
The final months of the project are dedicated to a global proteomic analysis of several melanoma cell lines regarding the oxidative-stress status at the molecular level. The focus is to screen the pathways involved the redox state of the epitope with potential clinical outcome in melanoma. Besides liquid chromatography and high-resolution mass spectrometry, other methods will also be used like siRNA silencing or CRISPR/Cas9 in order to effectively validate the findings.
